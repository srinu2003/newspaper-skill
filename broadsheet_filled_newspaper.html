City Council Approves New Riverside Park After Years of Debate
Long-delayed green space project finally breaks ground, promising 40 acres of public land by next spring
By A. Correspondent  |  Staff Reporter
[ Photograph — Riverside Park groundbreaking ceremony ]
City officials break ground on the long-awaited Riverside Park project Tuesday morning.

After nearly a decade of proposals, public hearings, and budget revisions, the City Council voted unanimously Tuesday to approve construction of a new 40-acre riverside park, marking one of the largest public land investments in the city's recent history.

The project, first proposed in 2018, faced repeated delays due to funding shortfalls and disputes over land use along the eastern riverbank. Officials say construction will begin this autumn, with the first phase expected to open to the public within eighteen months.

The park will include walking trails, a community amphitheater, and restored wetland habitat intended to reduce flooding in nearby neighborhoods. Local advocacy groups, who have pushed for the project since its inception, called the vote a long-overdue victory for the community.

Mayor's office representatives noted the project was made possible in part by a state infrastructure grant secured earlier this year, easing pressure on the municipal budget and allowing the plan to move forward without additional local taxes.
